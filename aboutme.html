I'm not like the other candidates.

TLDR; I'm the best candidate for the position. Just take my word for it.

Or keep reading to learn about the person behind the resume.

Some would say this is where you put the elevator pitch. If you're looking for that, feel free to peruse my resume. But for the next few lines of text, I want to tell you what makes me the best candidate.


I started my career at an agency, Markitors, supporting the growth of small businesses. Forged through fire, I juggled various niche clients, from healthcare and recruiting to hospitality and lifestyle.


If you know what they say about agency life, you'll know it's not for the faint of heart. Daily communication with international clients. Managing complex PPC budgets. Crafting multiplex segmented email campaigns. Writing articles, white papers, and ebooks. Tracking KPIs.


I did it all. And it made me a better version of myself. I can work in fast-paced environments, juggling competing priorities and navigating delicate client and team relationships.


I didn't stay a marketer for long. Soon after, I became a content marketing manager and then a senior content marketing manager and editor.


I pride myself in my written and spoken communication. I earned my Master of Arts in English to improve my communication, leadership, and critical thinking skills. The better I can communicate with my team, the more efficient we are. Likewise, the better I communicate with stakeholders, the better the product and the likelihood of converting target customers.


I'd be remiss to say I just edited for grammar, spelling, readability, AP style, and SEO, though. My greatest accomplishment is leading and mentoring a team of brilliant writers and freelancers.


In addition to being the senior content marketing manager and editor, I was my content team's Agile scrum master. I was the team liaison, project manager, and cheerleader in this role. We won together, or we lost together. But between you and me: I like to win and will do everything possible to support the team across the finish line!


This brings me to my most recent experience as a senior content marketing manager. The apex of my career thus far. If this were a Harry Potter movie, this is the silence right before Harry tumbles from Hagrid's arms on the Hogwarts battlefield.


All the skills acquired up until this point prepared me to:


  • Write dynamic strategies
  • Monitor KPIs
  • Write engaging content for a diverse audience
  • Maintain strict deadlines
  • Manage contributors

Lastly, here are two things you need to know about me:


  1. I'm resilient. Let's address the elephant in the room. My tenures at BestColleges and SKillcrush are quite short. Unfortunately, I was laid off twice in the last nine months. I don't take this lightly, and I know employers don't either. But I can assure you that I want to stay long-term at a company and contribute. While devastating, I've grown which leads me to my next point.

  2. I'm a lifelong learner. I believe in the power of education. As a first-generation college graduate, I know my knowledge is my power. This thesis is why I earned my Master's degree in English. It's also why I learned how to code. You didn't think this was a WordPress theme, did you? I taught myself HTML, CSS, and Javascript to build this site. The bottom line is that I can learn on the fly. Where my experience may "lack," I more than make up for it in gusto, and I'm excited to prove it!

Thank you for taking the time to read my candidacy story. I look forward to hearing from you soon.
